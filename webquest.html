Task 7: How Credit Affects Prices

Having a good credit score proves to lenders that you are a reliable borrower of money when you go to take out loans for cars, homes, or other items. Good credit scores increase the probability of you getting a low interest rate on a loan, which will make things cheaper to buy in the long run.

Julia and Larissa are two sisters who want to buy matching cars. Julia has missed a few payments on her credit card and as a result, has a credit score of 560. Larissa has never missed a payment on either of her credit cards, which she has had for 3 years, and has a credit score of 710.

The price of the car they want is $12,000.

The loan term is three years (36 months).

Julia has an interest rate of 8.4%.

Larissa, who has a better credit score, has an interest rate of 3.6%.

They both plan to put a $2,000 down payment.

The Trade-in value of the car is $0 and they are in the state of Pennsylvania with a 7% sales tax.

Total registration and other fees amount to $300.

Answer the following questions by using the calculator below.

What is Julia's total cost of buying his car?
What is Larissa's total cost of buying her car?
